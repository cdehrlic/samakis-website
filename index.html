Our Story

A hobby that crossed
borders and oceans

It started on the slopes of Mt. Kenya, where Simon Marrian was running safari camps. In his spare time, he picked up a hobby: smoking yellowfin tuna over wood fire. Before long, he was experimenting with every local variety he could find, and what began as a pastime earned an international reputation.

"Our all natural, artisanal smoked fish is not what we do, but who we are."

In 1983, Simon and his young family moved to the United States and settled in the Hudson Valley, operating out of a stone barn on their property. It was there that he built Samaki's signature brick kiln smokers — a design the company still uses today.

Simon's son Jason started working alongside his father as a teenager and has since taken the reins, carrying on the family tradition of exceptional hand-crafted smoked fish. Three generations. One standard.

The Craft

Slow methods.
No shortcuts.

01
Source
Only the finest all-natural ingredients. Wild Alaskan salmon, Irish organic Atlantic salmon, Great Lakes whitefish — sourced with care from trusted waters around the world.
02
Cure
Hand-cured in small batches with brown sugar and salt. No preservatives. No artificial coloring. Just patience and attention to craft.
03
Smoke
Smoked slowly in our signature brick kiln ovens — the same design Simon built in that stone barn decades ago. Natural oak, smoldering low and slow.
04
Finish
Hand-sliced and inspected. Every piece reflects the family standard. Ready for your table, your bagel, your Sunday brunch.
